Kazakhstan, located in the heart of Eurasia, has unique natural monuments, which are in harmony with historical buildings and modern architecture. Among Kazakhstan's mountains and steps, there are amazing places that sometimes take your breath away.

Almaty and Almaty region

Almaty is a city where foreign travelers often begin their acquaintance with Kazakhstan. The city is a place where foreign travelers often begin their acquaintance with Kazakhstan by visiting its cozy cafes, architectural monuments, and a variety of natural attractions, from arid deserts and picturesque canyons to beautiful mountains and lakes. And you can also look at the city from the highest hill - Kok-Tyube - by taking a cable car up there, go skating at the high-mountain Medeo rink and skiing at Shymbulak.

Charyn Canyon


Millions of years ago this place was part of a gigantic ancient sea stretching from present-day Lake Alakol to the Caspian Sea.

Charyn Canyon Castle Valley
The structure of the canyon walls there is rather soft; they are rocks, firmly compacted with clay, but easily change shape under the influence of wind and water. Fabulous and incredible in its beauty and uniqueness, it attracts tourists from all over the world.

Lakes Kolsay and Kaindy

The Kolsai Lakes are one of the most beautiful places in the world. They are rightfully called the blue pearl of the Tien Shan. In Kazakh, "kolsay" means "lake in the gorge. All three reservoirs of the same name are located in the same picturesque gorge, Kolsay, in the eastern part of the Kungei Alatau mountain range, which is 300 kilometers from Almaty.

Singing Barkhan of the "Altyn-Emel" Reserve

A 150-meter sand dune, standing alone in the desert near the Ili River, is the crown jewel of Kazakhstan's famous Altyn Emel Nature Reserve. The phenomenon of the dune lies in the unusual sounds it emits when the wind blows. Scientists explain its singing by the fact that when the weather is dry enough, when the sand is slowly falling down, the sand grains rub, causing them to vibrate and produce sounds similar to an organ.

Nur-Sultan


Nur-Sultan (formerly Astana, Akmola) is the capital of Kazakhstan. In 23 years, the city with a population of 280,000 people has become a millionaire.
The capital has intertwined old buildings from the Soviet period and Art Nouveau, with masterpieces by foreign architects, such as the Palace of Independence, the Pyramid, Khan Shatyr, Astana Triumph, Baiterek, and so on. All these buildings give a special flavor and charm to the northern capital.
